    Skills

    Methodical troubleshooting / problem solving mindset

    Inevitably, while performing high-throughput lab testing, I would sometimes get a result that didn't make sense in context. I learned to look for patterns in the data that could implicate the steps at which errors could have occurred and the equipment that might have malfunctioned, and to then run through the corresponding potential fixes.

    I've carried this mindset over into coding. I methodically use tools to troublshoot, including browser dev tools to investigate element placement, and, when needed, online searches because consulting others is more efficient than re-inventing the wheel.

    Attention to detail, combined with solid mental overview of concurrent projects

    Details are impactful. In the lab, I honed my sharp attention to detail while being mindful of multiple details at each of the steps in the workflow: remember to add chemical X, but only if I'm working with species Y, and only if using variant Z of the method; if the robot's liquid levels are off at point A in the process, check the output for effect B; etc.

    Concurrently, I learned to be aware of overarching projects' needs. If priorities for a project shifted, I was able to mentally pivot on the fly, re-schedule various tasks for multiple projects to maintain respective timelines, and reach out to colleagues where needed to ensure all tasks were performed on time.

    I've found that experience invaluable in web development. Attention to detail has helped me create responsive and visually pleasing layouts. At the same time, having a mental model of an overall project has been instrumental in efficiently isolating which lines of code to alter.

    Excellent communication skills: concise, keeping in mind what others need to know and antipating what questions they might need answered

    Over time I've noticed coworkers asking me the same questions in the same contexts. It's helped me to further develop my theory of mind. I now habitually ask myself: what might this person need to know, how can I present the information in a way that makes sense to them as they hear about it for the first time, and what follow-up questions might I have if I was in their position? I also do my best to actively listen, address any apparent concerns, and focus on sharing relevant information.

    These are communication skills that translate to any workplace. If you ask any of my references, I'm confident they'll describe me as easy and fun to work with, and that they'll verify my ability to communicate effectively.

    Understanding the importance of taking time to plan before starting work, to save time later and ensure effective collaboration

    In the past, I let impatience and enthusiasm get the better of me and didn't want to "waste" time planning before getting to the "actual" work. However, as my manager will tell you, I have made huge progress over the years. I understand now that I've learned the hard way: a little planning at the outset can save time spent on unnecessary tasks or irrelevant side quests.

    Once begun is half done, but only if you know where you're going.
